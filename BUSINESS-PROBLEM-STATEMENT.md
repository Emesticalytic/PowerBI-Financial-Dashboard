# Business Problem & Executive Analytics Need: ABC Bank Case Study

## The Business Challenge

ABC Bank faced a critical strategic problem in 2024: **rapid revenue growth masked underlying inefficiencies and missed profitability targets.**

Despite impressive year-over-year growth of **38.86%**, the organization was operating largely in the dark:

### Key Problems Identified

**1. Revenue Growth Without Profitability Clarity**
- Total revenue: £214.57M
- Total profit: £142.86M
- Revenue target: £249.28M (missed by £34.71M annually)
- Problem: Leadership didn't know if they were truly profitable or if they were chasing vanity metrics

**2. Invisible Cost Structures**
- Professional Services category running at 60% cost-to-revenue ratio
- No visibility into category-level profitability
- Unable to identify cost optimization opportunities
- Budget variance of -£34.71M (actual vs. target unknown without clear tracking)

**3. Geographic Blindness**
- North America dominating (35% of revenue, £74M)
- Africa representing only 4% (£9M)
- No understanding of market opportunity gaps
- Could not identify where to invest for growth
- No market tier segmentation (Core vs. Growth vs. Emerging markets)

**4. Regional Performance Chaos**
- Six regions reporting, but regional profitability not understood
- Revenue-to-profit conversion different by region but invisible
- Unable to benchmark regional performance against targets
- Middle East at 65.58% margin, but why? No analysis

**5. Customer & Segment Mystery**
- Selling to 5 customer segments (SMB, Mid-Market, Enterprise, Startup, Government)
- No visibility into which segments were profitable
- Top 10 customers generating £54M but relationship value unknown
- Could not allocate resources to high-value vs. time-consuming customers

**6. Channel Effectiveness Unknown**
- 5 sales channels (Direct Sales, Partner, Online, Reseller, Inbound)
- No data on which channels had best ROI
- Marketing spend allocated without performance data
- Could not optimize go-to-market strategy

**7. Product Mix Blindness**
- 4 product categories: Software (£136M), Professional Services (£48M), Cloud Services (£25M), Support (£6M)
- No category-level profitability analysis
- Software driving revenue but was it most profitable?
- Unable to guide product development strategy

**8. Revenue Target Failure (Chronic)**
- Continuously missing revenue targets by ~13.9%
- Targets set without data-driven methodology
- No forecasting capability
- Led to board-level questions about execution and planning

**9. Growth Deceleration Unexplained**
- Growth peaked in 2022 at 49%
- Declined to 20% in 2023, then 6% in 2024
- No explanation for why growth was decelerating
- Executives didn't know if this was normal maturation or execution problem

**10. Strategic Decision-Making in Vacuum**
- Which markets to enter next?
- Should we expand Professional Services or focus on Software?
- Which channels to invest in?
- How to allocate sales teams across regions?
- **All answered by gut feel, not data**

## Why Executives Needed Analytical Insights

### The C-Suite Perspective

**CEO Challenges:**
- Board asking: "Why did we miss targets by £34.71M?"
- Investor pressure: "What's your growth strategy for 2025?"
- Market expansion: "Where should we expand internationally?"
- **Need:** One dashboard showing all revenue, profit, and growth metrics in context

**CFO Challenges:**
- Budget variance of -£34.71M raised red flags
- Gross margin of 66.58% looked good, but by category?
- Cost control unclear: Professional Services at 60% COGS ratio
- **Need:** Deep dive into profitability by region, category, customer

**VP Sales Challenges:**
- £249.28M target seems arbitrary (why this number?)
- Sales team arguing targets are unachievable
- Regional managers claiming they're performing well but metrics unclear
- **Need:** Regional benchmarking, target realism, channel ROI

**VP Product Challenges:**
- Which product category should get more R&D investment?
- Is Software really the growth driver?
- Why is Cloud Services growing slower than expected?
- **Need:** Category-level revenue and margin trends

**Head of Finance/Planning:**
- Forecast for 2025 was guesswork
- Budget planning happening without actual data
- Growth assumptions outdated (using 2022's 49% growth rate?)
- **Need:** Time-series forecasting with confidence intervals

## The Analytical Solution: 6-Page Dashboard

ABC Bank built this dashboard to directly address these gaps:

### Page 1: Sales Performance
**Executive Need:** "Show me where we're making money"
- Actual vs. targeted revenue by region (reveals North America gap of £12M)
- Top countries by profit (reveals that Canada ≠ highest profit driver)
- Regional revenue breakdown with margins (shows Africa at 66.95% vs. 65.58% average)
- Revenue trends (shows 4-year trajectory and seasonality)
**Business Impact:** Executives see immediately that North America has execution gap, while Africa punches above weight on margins

### Page 2: Executive Overview
**Executive Need:** "What's my one-page snapshot?"
- Regional performance table (one click shows all metrics for any region)
- Top 10 customers (shows Advanced at £7.20M vs. competitors)
- Orders by segment (Enterprise: 2.7K, SMB: 5.3K - volume vs. value trade-off)
- Revenue by channel breakdown (Partner channel at 34.73% is largest, Direct Sales at 12.05%)
- Revenue by category (Software dominance clear at 63%)
**Business Impact:** Board presentations now have authoritative single source of truth

### Page 3: Financial Analysis
**Executive Need:** "How profitable are we really?"
- Profit target vs. actual (£163.88M target, £142.86M actual = -£34.71M variance)
- Margin target achieved (65.74% target, 66.58% actual = exceeding expectations)
- Cost by category (Professional Services at £29.25M reveals cost center)
- Budget vs. actual tracking (shows monthly trends and seasonality)
- P&L breakdown showing revenue growth drivers
**Business Impact:** CFO can now explain to board why targets missed (ambitious target-setting, not execution failure)

### Page 4: Global Map
**Executive Need:** "Where should we expand?"
- Interactive world map (visual of revenue concentration in North America)
- Top 5 countries (US, Canada, Mexico all North America - concentration risk!)
- Market tier analysis (Tier 1 Core: 54.98%, Tier 2 Growth: 34.47%, Tier 3 Emerging: 10.55%)
- Opportunity scores (identifies under-exploited markets)
**Business Impact:** CEO can now make data-driven expansion decisions; identifies Asia Pacific as growth opportunity

### Page 5: AI Insights
**Executive Need:** "What actually drives our revenue?"
- Key influencers identified (Core Banking Platform is top revenue driver at +£1.29M)
- Segment analysis (Enterprise is highest value per order, SMB is volume driver)
- Channel performance (Direct Sales + Partner channels outperform)
- What-if scenarios ("If we focus on Software, revenue increases by X%")
- Revenue breakdown drill-down (Category → Region → Country → Channel → Segment)
**Business Impact:** Product team now knows Software + Core Banking Platform is winning formula; sales team sees channel ROI

### Page 6: Forecast
**Executive Need:** "What will 2025 revenue look like?"
- YTD tracking (£60.04M progress against annual targets)
- YoY growth trends (explains 2022 peak at 49%, now 6% due to base effect)
- Revenue forecast model (projects £7M-£8M monthly in 2025)
- Growth narrative (explains deceleration is maturation, not failure)
- Budget variance historical (explains target-setting issues)
**Business Impact:** Planning team can now set realistic 2025 targets; investors see credible growth outlook

## Specific Business Outcomes from Analytics

### Before Dashboard
-  Target-setting was guesswork (consistently missed by £34.71M)
-  Regional performance opaque (couldn't explain why North America underperforming)
-  Customer profitability unknown (top 10 customers might include low-margin accounts)
-  Channel ROI invisible (spending equally on underperforming channels)
-  Growth trajectory unclear (didn't understand if 6% decline was normal or alarming)
-  Cost control weak (Professional Services at 60% cost ratio, no optimization)
-  Market expansion decisions made by politics, not data
-  Board presentations used anecdotes, not facts

### After Dashboard
- **Data-Driven Target Setting:** 2025 targets now based on forecast model (£249.28M → £280M-£300M projected, targets set more realistically)
-  **Regional Accountability:** North America exec now has clear £12M gap to explain
-  **Customer Strategy:** Top 10 customers clearly identified; relationship management prioritized
-  **Channel Optimization:** Direct Sales (12%) vs. Partner (34.73%) ROI now comparable
- **Growth Understanding:** Leadership explains 2022-2024 arc is natural maturation, projects 2025 growth at 15-20% (realistic)
- **Cost Management:** Professional Services cost ratio targeted for reduction; initiatives identified
- **Market Expansion:** Asia Pacific identified as growth opportunity; geographic diversification strategy underway
- **Board Confidence:** Quarterly board presentations now use comprehensive analytics, not estimates

## Conclusion: Why This Dashboard Was Essential

ABC Bank's executive team needed this dashboard because:

1. **Growth without insight is dangerous** — 38.86% growth looked good but masked inefficiencies
2. **Target failure erodes credibility** — Missing by £34.71M annually needed explanation
3. **Strategic decisions require data** — Market expansion, product mix, channel strategy all need analytics
4. **Cost control requires visibility** — Professional Services at 60% COGS impossible to address without segmentation
5. **Forecasting prevents surprises** — 2025 planning needed data-driven projections, not guesses
6. **Board accountability demands transparency** — One dashboard better than multiple conflicting reports

This 6-page dashboard transformed ABC Bank from a growth-at-all-costs organization to a **data-driven, profitable, strategically focused enterprise.**

The insights enabled decisions that:
-  Made 2025 targets realistic and achievable
-  Identified £48M+ of optimization opportunity
-  Enabled geographic expansion strategy
- Improved profitability by understanding cost structures
- Restored board and investor confidence
